WELCOME TO INTERACTIVE MEDIA

Human-Computer Interaction/Interfaces

HCI is where human meets technology, our senses colliding with that of the computer in a mutuality of action and reaction. This interaction with technology focuses on design, implementation, usability, efficiency, and user experience.



HCI can be classified in these ways:

SENSE

Interactions that enhance our natural senses. For example, these pedal bins that when open upon being stepped on play a symphony.

NONSENSE

Interactions that invert and play with our senses. Take this mouse that pops its head out of a hole and speaks to us with the voice of a little girl's for example.

SYNAESTHESIA

Interactions where one sense translates into another sense. Like these grills that allow its user to listen to music through their teeth.
